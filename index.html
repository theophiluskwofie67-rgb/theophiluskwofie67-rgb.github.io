Research

My work lies at the intersection of mathematical modeling, machine learning, and public and population health, with an emphasis on interpretable and trustworthy AI.

Research interests

I develop and analyze compartmental and mechanistic models of complex health systems, combining them with data-driven and machine learning approaches to inform decision-making under uncertainty.

Machine learning for public health Interpretable & trustworthy AI Compartmental modeling Hybrid mechanistic/data-driven models Uncertainty quantification Optimization & control Health equity Digital health & epidemiology

Current themes

  • Modeling the dynamics of social behavior and their impact on health outcomes (e.g., obesity, substance use, crime).
  • Quantifying the effects of interventions (e.g., testing, isolation compliance, vector control) in epidemic and endemic disease settings.
  • Optimal control and cost-effectiveness of public health interventions, including fear-based deterrence in crime dynamics.
  • Linking mechanistic models with modern ML pipelines to support robust, fair, and interpretable prediction in data-limited populations.
